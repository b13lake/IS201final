Nikola Tesla was born July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Austrian Empire or what is now modern-day Croatia. He attended school at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz and also the Charles-Ferdinand University in Prague, although he never obtained a degree. Of Nikola Tesla's numerous efforts, he is most known for the alternating current (AC) electric system. Tesla died January 7th, 1943 in New York City.

  1. Electric Magnetic Motor

    • Electric motor which creates mechanical energy from electrical energy.
    • Utilizes electromagnetism in order to function.
    • This motor is seen in everyday life, from common household items, to industrial machines.



  2. Electrical Transmission of Power

    • Transports electricity from one location to another.
    • Uses high-volage power lines to reduce loss of power through long distances.
    • Essential in transporting power from plants to everyday consumers.
  1. Electric Circuit Controller

    • A device utilized to direct the flow of electricity in a conductive circuit.
    • Can be a switch, relay, or other control devices.
    • Critical in managing electrical functions.



  2. Dynamo Electric Machine

    • An early generator that produces direct current (DC).
    • Takes mechanical energy and converts it to electrical energy through use of electrical coils.
    • Was a landmark product in the field of electrical generation.
  1. System of Electrical Distribution

    • An organized plan of distribution of electricity from producer to consumer.
    • Utilized transformers, substations, and distribution lines.
    • Created an efficient and reliable method of electricity transportation.
